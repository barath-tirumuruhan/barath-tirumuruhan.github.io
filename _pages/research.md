## Optical Modeling of OLED Devices — Ferry Group, UMN (2025–present)

Thin-film optoelectronic devices like OLEDs rely on precise control of optical interference, emission zone placement, and light extraction efficiency. Small changes in layer thickness or refractive index can have outsized effects on device performance.

My work uses **Lumerical FDTD and STACK** alongside **MATLAB** to:
- Model optical interference and emission profiles in multilayer OLED stacks
- Identify design parameters that maximize outcoupling efficiency
- Collaborate with experimental collaborators to validate simulation predictions against fabricated devices
- Guide iterative design improvements before costly fabrication runs

This project is industry-sponsored and conducted under NDA; a manuscript is in preparation for submission in summer 2026.

**Relevant skills:** Lumerical (FDTD & STACK), MATLAB, thin-film optics, transfer matrix method, DOE, optical characterization

## Nanoparticle Barcoding Platform — Boehnke Group, UMN (2022–2025)

Lipid-coated nanoparticles can be chemically functionalized to carry unique "barcodes" detectable via mass spectrometry, enabling multiplexed diagnostics at the cellular level.

My contributions to this platform included:
- Streamlining synthesis and bioanalytical workflows to improve reproducibility
- Developing and prototyping new in vitro cell assay protocols from scratch — integrating conjugation, formulation, extraction, and GC-MS analysis
- Investigating nanoparticle–tumor cell interactions to guide design of next-generation diagnostic tools

**Relevant skills:** Nanoparticle formulation, GC-MS, HPLC, UV-Vis/fluorescence spectroscopy, cell culture, bioanalytical chemistry

## Battery Lifetime Prediction — IISc Bangalore (2021–2022)

Under Dr. Naga Phani B. Aetukuri at the Indian Institute of Science, I built a Coulometry-based framework for predicting lithium-ion battery lifetime from early-cycle charge/discharge data.

- Automated charge-discharge data collection and analysis using Python and LabView
- Designed and prototyped custom pressure-cell assemblies to test garnet-type solid electrolytes under controlled stack pressures
- Developed reliability models connecting early cycling behavior to long-term degradation

**Relevant skills:** Electrochemistry, CV, impedance spectroscopy, Python, LabView, SolidWorks

## Composite Materials Optimization — BITS Pilani (2019–2021)

Under Dr. Sujith R at BITS Pilani, I worked on two concurrent projects:

**Boron carbide reinforced aluminum alloy for ballistic armor** — Applied Design of Experiments (DOE) and Taguchi analysis to optimize process parameters (sintering temperature, pressure, reinforcement fraction) for mechanical performance. This work led to a peer-reviewed publication in *Journal of Materials Engineering and Performance*.

**Silicon-based anodes for high-capacity Li-ion batteries** — Engineered silicon and silicon oxycarbide anode materials, investigating capacity, cycling stability, and failure mechanisms.

**Relevant skills:** DOE, Taguchi methods, powder metallurgy, electrochemical characterization
