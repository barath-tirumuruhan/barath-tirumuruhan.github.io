I am a 4th-year PhD candidate in [Materials Science & Engineering](https://cse.umn.edu/cems) at the [University of Minnesota Twin Cities](https://twin-cities.umn.edu/), advised by [Prof. Vivian Ferry](https://ferrygroup.umn.edu/). My research focuses on optical modeling and optimization of thin-film optoelectronic devices, with an emphasis on OLED design.

I use Lumerical (FDTD & STACK) and MATLAB to model optical interference, emission profiles, and light extraction in multilayer thin-film stacks — working in close collaboration with experimental teams to translate simulations into actionable design improvements. My current work is part of an industry-sponsored project under NDA targeting next-generation OLED products.

Before joining the Ferry Group, I spent three years developing a nanoparticle barcoding platform for cancer diagnostics in the [Boehnke Group](https://boehnkelab.umn.edu/), where I built bioanalytical workflows, in vitro cell assay protocols, and GC-MS analysis pipelines. This pivot from biomedical nanomaterials to optoelectronics reflects a deliberate choice to build depth across experimental and computational materials science.

My broader research interests include:
- Physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) for optical and materials modeling
- Design of Experiments (DOE) and statistical optimization in materials R&D
- Optical scattering in paint coatings and soft matter systems *(upcoming project)*

Prior to UMN, I completed a dual degree (M.Sc. Chemistry + B.E. Mechanical Engineering) at [BITS Pilani](https://www.bits-pilani.ac.in/), and interned at the [Indian Institute of Science (IISc)](https://iisc.ac.in/), where I developed a coulometry-based battery lifetime prediction framework.

I am available for **summer 2026 internships** and **full-time positions from October 2027**.  
I am an Indian national on F-1 visa with 3-year STEM OPT eligibility, and am open to positions in both the US and Europe.
